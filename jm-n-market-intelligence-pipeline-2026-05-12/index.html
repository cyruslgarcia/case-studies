Market Intelligence Pipeline

An automated market research tool built with Python, the Claude API, and Airtable: designed to surface structured competitive intelligence from unstructured web content at near-zero marginal cost.

Problem

My co-founder at Green Think Energy came up through Solar City, now part of Tesla, with a practiced eye for market movement: acquisitions, new entrants, shifts in competitive positioning. This kind of market intelligence paid real dividends. I saw it firsthand: market awareness shaped how we navigated Green Think Energy's acquisition by Enphase Energy, and informed how we identified and approached partnership opportunities that expanded our market reach. I wanted a reading companion that would do what a sharp analyst does instinctively: flag the key companies and people in every article, map the relationships between them, and keep a running record of even minor players so nothing gets lost when they resurface.

The pipeline ingests an article URL, strips markup via BeautifulSoup, and passes clean text to a purpose-built extraction prompt. Claude returns a structured JSON object containing entity extraction (companies, people), relationship tagging (investor, acquirer, partner), and classification across a custom 37-tag taxonomy. Results write into a four-table relational schema in Airtable.

Key design decisions

Model selection

Chose Sonnet over Haiku after testing both. Haiku's lower cost didn't justify the degraded entity extraction and inconsistent tag application on ambiguous articles.

Taxonomy design

Customer-facing description and internal delivery mechanism are different things. A company can market itself as "AI-powered" while operating as professional services. Keeping Market Position and Operating Model as separate fields required a 37-tag system across 10 groups to hold that distinction consistently.

Cost optimization

Combined BeautifulSoup pre-processing with prompt caching to bring per-article cost from ~$0.30–0.42 to ~$0.05: over 85% reduction without sacrificing output quality.
