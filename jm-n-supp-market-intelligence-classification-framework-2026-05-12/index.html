Background

Classification systems fail at the edges. The easy cases tag cleanly: a bank, a SaaS company, a VC fund. The hard cases reveal whether the system was designed to think or just to sort.

The edge case that shaped this taxonomy was my own company. Green Think Energy (GTE) delivered solar permit plan sets. Customers experienced it as a professional service governed by SLAs supporting tight turnaround times. Internally, it ran as a technology-enabled production operation supported by custom databases, clearly defined inputs, and standardized outputs. One tag could not hold both descriptions without losing something true.

The solution was splitting the question in two. Market Position and Operating Model became separate fields. What a customer buys and how a company actually delivers value are not the same question, and collapsing them into one answer produces a classification that is accurate about neither.

The core distinction: Green Think Energy

Field Question answered Green Think Energy
Market Position What does the customer buy? Professional Services
Operating Model How is value actually delivered? Tech-enabled Services
Vertical What industry does it serve? Energy

A company marketing itself as a service business and a company running a production operation are different things. In the case of GTE, both were true simultaneously, on different sides of the same transaction. The taxonomy holds that distinction rather than forcing a choice.

Classification architecture

Market Position

What does the customer see and buy?

The market-facing product or service. How the company is perceived and positioned externally.

Operating Model

How is value created and delivered?

The internal delivery mechanism. May match Market Position or diverge significantly from it.

Vertical

What industry does it serve?

The sector the company operates within. Separate from what it sells or how it delivers.

Design principles

AI-powered is a modifier, never a primary tag

AI-powered must always be paired with a primary classification. It describes how something is built, not what it is. A company cannot be classified as AI-powered alone any more than a restaurant can be classified as "gas-fired."

Correct: AI Platform / Tooling + AI-powered    Incorrect: AI-powered alone

Null is a valid output

If an article provides insufficient information to classify a field with confidence, the field is left null. Guessing produces noise that compounds across every downstream query. A structured blank is more useful than a confident error.

Applied to: Operating Model, Vertical, Business Line when article evidence is insufficient

Native vs. General in Vertical

General means a company serves all industries with no dominant sector. Native means the company constitutes the industry itself rather than serving it. Visa is Native to Finance because Visa is financial infrastructure. Salesforce is General because it serves every sector. The distinction matters for how a market position is actually interpreted.

Native: Visa, Mastercard    General: Salesforce, AWS
